# Reconnaissance

Reconnaissance is the research and planning of an attack against a system or victim. It is often passive and undetected.

## OSINT

**OSINT** - With OSINT (Open-Source Intelligence), adversaries gather insights about their target through publicly available information. 

## Reconnaissance Types

- **Passive Recon** - This involves having no direct interaction with the target. This may be WHOIS lookup, social media scraping or reviewing breach data.
- **Active Recon** - This involves direct contact with the target with activities such as social engineering, port scanning, banner grabbing or probing for open services.


**Email Harvesting** - This is the process of obtaining email addresses from public, paid or free services. 

# Weaponization

This is the process of creating actionable attack tools by crafting malware and exploits into a payload.
> **Malware** - is a program or software that is designed to damage, disrupt or gain unauthorised access to a computer.
> **Exploits** - are programs or code that take advantage of the vulnerability or flaw in the application or system.
> **Payload** - is a malicious code that the attacker runs on the system.

# Delivery

This is the method for transmitting the payload or the malware onto the targets environment. There are plenty of options for this which include: *Phishing email*, *USB drops*, *Watering hole attacks*, etc.

# Exploitation

This is when the attacker's code executes on the target, taking advantage of a known vulnerability. 

## Key Techniques for Exploitation

- **Malicious macro execution** - This may have been delivered through a phishing email, that would execute ransomware when the victim opens it.
- **Zero-day exploits** - These leverages on unknown and unpatched flaws in a system. These exploits leave no opportunity for detection at the beginning.
- **Known CVEs** - The attacker can choose to exploit unpatched public vulnerabilities found on the target environment.
